# Devin

## Company Overview

Built by Cognition, founded 2023 in San Francisco. Founding team includes competitive programming champions (Scott Wu, CEO). Raised $400M at $10.2B valuation (Sep 2025 Series C, Founders Fund). 49 employees mid-2025. Acquired Windsurf mid-2025. Revenue grew from $1M ARR (Sep 2024) to $80M (Oct 2025) -- 73x growth in 13 months. Devin produces 25% of Cognition's own codebase.

## Product/Service Offering

Positioned as the "world's first AI software engineer." Fully autonomous: sandboxed workspace with shell, editor, browser, and persistent memory. Plans, codes, tests, debugs, and deploys without real-time intervention.

**Devin 2.0 (Apr 2025):** 83% more tasks per ACU, 4x faster, 2x more efficient. PR merge rate rose from 34% to 67%. Sub-agent spawning for parallel work.

**Enterprise integrations:** Slack, GitHub, JIRA. Custom Devins for team-specific workflows.

## Pricing Strategy

| Tier | Cost | ACUs Included |
|------|------|---------------|
| Individual | $20/mo min | 150 (~$2.25/ACU) |
| Team | $500/mo | 250 |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

Down from $500/mo entry price pre-2.0. ACU consumption is opaque; performance reportedly degrades after ~10 ACUs per session.

## Marketing & Messaging

Framed as replacing junior developer headcount. "Your AI software engineer." "Hybrid workforce." "Never sleeps." Case studies: Goldman Sachs pilot claims output equivalent to 14,400 from 12,000 humans; Nubank reports 20x cost savings on migrations.

## Market Perception

### Strengths

- Fastest ARR growth in the space ($1M to $73M in a year)
- $10.2B valuation reflects investor confidence
- Marquee enterprise customers (Goldman Sachs, Santander, Nubank)
- Devin 2.0 delivered meaningful improvements
- Excels at bounded tasks (scraping, migrations, ETL)
- Windsurf acquisition broadens product offering

### Weaknesses

- Independent testing: ~15% success rate (3/20 tasks)
- Trustpilot rating: 3.0/5
- ACU consumption opaque and hard to predict
- Cannot handle ambiguity well
- Gets stuck in loops on complex tasks
- Not a substitute for senior engineers
- Gap between marketing claims and reality (The Register: "bad at its job")
