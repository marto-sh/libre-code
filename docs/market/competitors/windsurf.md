# Windsurf

## Company Overview

Originally Codeium (Exafunction Inc), founded June 2021 by Varun Mohan and Douglas Chen (MIT). Raised $243M total at a $1.25B valuation (August 2024). Revenue reached $82M ARR at time of acquisition. 350+ enterprise customers. Ranked #1 on LogRocket AI Dev Tool Rankings (February 2026).

**Acquisition turbulence:** An OpenAI $3B acquisition collapsed due to Microsoft partnership conflicts. Google executed a "reverse acquihire" for $2.4B (hired the CEO and 40 engineers). Cognition then acquired the remaining Windsurf assets for approximately $250M (July 2025). Windsurf is now owned by Cognition, which is valued at $10.2B.

## Product/Service Offering

VS Code fork with deeply integrated AI capabilities.

**Key features:**

- **Cascade:** Agentic assistant with Chat and Code modes, featuring a built-in planning agent that separates long-term and short-term planning
- **"Flows" paradigm:** Hybrid copilot + agent with persistent context awareness
- **Inline completion** (Tab)
- **Context engine** with configurable Rules and Memories
- **Devin integration** in progress (post-Cognition acquisition)

Enterprise customers report 40-200% productivity gains.

## Marketing & Messaging

"The first agentic IDE." Targets professional and enterprise engineers. Emphasizes flow state preservation. Explicitly contrasts against Copilot's plugin approach — Windsurf positions itself as a re-engineered IDE, not a bolt-on. Notable enterprise customers include Zillow, Dell, and Anduril.

## Market Perception

### Strengths

- Cascade multi-file editing is reliable and well-regarded
- Cleaner UI than Cursor
- Deep codebase awareness via context engine
- Strong enterprise adoption
- Competitive free tier
- Devin integration roadmap (via Cognition ownership)

### Weaknesses

- Performance inconsistency on complex codebases
- Credit billing opacity
- Support response delays
- Acquisition turbulence: founding leadership has departed
- Steeper learning curve than alternatives
- Described as "uneven execution" by reviewers
